Things to Do

Fun activities to undertake by yourself, with family, friends, or significant others. Check out all that Pittsford has to offer!

Fun Fact:

Pittsford is a village in Monroe County, New York, United States. The population was 1,355 at the 2010 census. It is named after Pittsford, Vermont, the native town of a founding father. The Village of Pittsford is the oldest village in New York incorporated in 1827.

Take a Boat Ride

Sam Patch takes you on a guided tour of one of the most famous canals in the United States. If you are lucky, you may see the Pittsford Rowing Team practicing their strokes next to the boat. After the tour, make sure to feed the ducks along the canal with some duck food that you can purchase for $1 in the local stores.

Go Rafting

Looking for something more high adrenaline? Check out the white water rafting course located right above Lock 32, right next to the Erie Canal. Open during the summer months, the skilled employees will equipt you with all of the gear and teach you the basics. Great way to spend some time in the hot summer weather!

Feed Some Fish

Take in this historic park in the middle of town. After hiking the trails, stop by the Fish Hatchery to feed some huge carp that the park breeds for the local rivers. Watch as they jump out of the water fighting for the food pellets.Check out some other picturesque hikes including Mendon Ponds, and Daffodil Meadow.

Listen to Live Music

Every Friday afternoon in the summer months, bands come together to play on the Erie Canal. Hear the tunes echo along the water, and enjoy the warm atmosphere. Don't forget to check out Paddle and Pour, a free event open to the public where a rowing regatta is combined with live music and food. This fun Pittsford event usually happens at the end of May.

Attend a Winter Fest

Candlelight night is a winter holiday celebration in the heart of the town, activities include live music, carolers, horse-drawn wagon rides, visits with Santa, treats with Mrs. Claus, and the lighting of Village Christmas trees. Many merchants offer holiday treats and special sale items as well. Check out the Pittsford Chamber website for more details about the event.

Visit an Old Farm

Visit the Tinker Homstead and Farm Museum close to the center of the Village, where you can see a family farm from the 1800s. Actively learn about native animals and insects in the area through various interactive displays. Every year, this park holds fairy house building and civil war reanactments, as well. A perfect sunny-day activity for kids and adults alike!
